Observational Techniques in Astronomy

Curated resources for learning and teaching observational techniques in astronomy. Specifically, this focuses on classes targeted at upper-level undergraduates, usually those majoring or minoring in astronomy. Most of the courses taught in this context teach how to use a telescope, how to plan an observation, and how to obtain and analyze data. Most of those I've found focus on CCD astronomy, but some also include spectroscopy and radio astronomy.

Resources

Course list: Observational Astronomy (undergraduate, in-person)

  • AST3722 — Public Materials: Public repository of course materials and notebooks for observational astronomy.
  • AST4723 — Public Materials (UF): Upper-level course on astronomical observations; optical and radio spectroscopy and data analysis with Jupyter notebooks.
  • Rice University Observatory: Observatory information, resources, and observing activities at Rice University. https://www.ruf.rice.edu/~ruco/users.html is the important link, but it's down at the moment.
  • PHY217 — Observational Astronomy (Sheffield): Lecture notes, labs, and resources on observational methods.
